The Playbook

The product sells itself — your job is to get it in front of the right person at the right time. Focus on understanding their business first. The website conversation happens naturally once you know what they need.

1

Do Your Homework

Before you walk in or pick up the phone, Google their business. Pull up their site on your phone. Check Yelp, read a few reviews. Know what they're working with so you can have a real conversation — not a cold pitch.

2

Start a Conversation, Not a Pitch

Find something genuine to mention — their work, their reviews, the business itself. People can tell when you're reading a script. Then ask something simple: "When someone around here searches for [their service], are you showing up the way you want to?"

3

Listen for the Gap

Ask questions and actually listen. "How are new customers finding you?" ... "Are you getting the kind of leads you want?" Most business owners know they should be doing more online — they just haven't had the time or found someone they trust. Let them tell you the problem.

4

Show Them What's Possible

Pull up a demo site on your phone and hand it to them. Don't oversell — just let them scroll through it. The quality speaks for itself. Then: "We can build something like this with your info, your reviews, your branding — ready in 48 hours."

5

Make It Easy to Say Yes

Most agencies charge $3-5K. We start at $699. Mention that context — it helps people understand the value. Start with Starter for anyone on the fence. If they're interested in ongoing growth, introduce the Growth plan as the next step. Pro is for businesses that want a full digital partner.

6

Handle Objections Honestly

"I have a Facebook page" — "That's a good start. The difference is you don't control who sees your posts — Facebook does. A website is something you own, and it shows up when people are actively searching for what you do."

"It's too expensive" — "I get that. Think about what one new customer is worth to you. For most of our clients, the site pays for itself with the first couple of leads."

"I need to think about it" — "No pressure at all. I can put together a free preview with your actual business info — takes me a few minutes. That way you can see exactly what you'd get before deciding."

"My nephew / a friend can build one" — "That could work. What we do differently is build specifically for search rankings and mobile conversion — the stuff that actually brings in customers. Happy to show you the difference."

7

Close and Capture

When they're ready, grab their info on the spot — name, number, email, business details. Use the Lead form right from your phone. The more detail you collect now, the faster we can build their preview and get them live.

Sample Script

Use this as a starting point. Make it your own — the best conversations don't sound scripted.

Opening "Hey, I was looking at [business name] — really like what you guys are doing with [genuine detail]. I'm with Polaris Point, we build websites for local businesses here in Austin. Quick question — when someone Googles '[their service] near me,' are you happy with where you're showing up?" If They Don't Have a Website "You'd be surprised how common that is — business owners are busy running their business. The tricky part is that most of your customers are searching online before they call anyone. A website makes sure those searches lead to you instead of someone else. We can have something up in 48 hours — want to see what it'd look like?" If They Have an Outdated Site "I took a look at your current site — honestly your business deserves better than what's showing up, especially on mobile. First impressions happen fast. We can replace it with something that actually reflects the quality of what you do. Let me show you a quick example..." Showing the Demo [Pull up a demo on your phone and hand it to them.] "This is one we built for a [similar business]. See how the reviews are right up front, click-to-call is right there, and the whole thing is built to turn a visitor into a phone call. We'd build yours with your info, your branding — same quality." The Close "The starter package is $699, one-time — no monthly fees, no contract. We handle the design, the copy, the SEO. You just approve it and we push it live. Want to get started?"

Things to Remember

  • Demo on your phone. That's how their customers will see it.
  • After you state the price, stop talking. Give them a second to process.
  • Collect info on the spot. Don't let a warm lead go cold because you said "I'll follow up."
  • Follow up within 24 hours. Send them the preview link — it does the selling overnight.
  • A "no" today is a "maybe" in 30 days. Add them as a Cold lead and circle back later.
  • The free preview is your best tool. Low commitment, high impact. Use it.
