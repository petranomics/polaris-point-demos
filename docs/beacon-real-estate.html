Beacon for Real Estate

A complete AI marketing system for agents, brokers, and teams. Every feature built around how real estate professionals actually work — listings, leads, neighborhoods, and relationships.

Why Real Estate Is Perfect for Beacon

Content-Hungry Industry

Agents need a constant stream of content — listing posts, market updates, neighborhood guides, open house promos, just-sold celebrations, buyer tips, seller tips. Most produce a fraction of what they should.

Relationship-Driven

Past client nurturing is everything. The agent who stays top-of-mind gets the referral. But most agents stop communicating after closing. Beacon automates the "stay in touch" problem.

Hyper-Local

Real estate is neighborhood-by-neighborhood. Generic content is useless. Beacon writes about specific neighborhoods, school districts, price trends, and local businesses — because it knows the agent's farm area.

High LTV

Average agent commission: $8,000-15,000 per transaction. One extra deal per year from better marketing = 10-30x ROI on Beacon. The math is obvious.

Scheduled Content (Runs Without Prompting)

Weekly — Monday

Social Media Content Pack

5 posts ready to schedule:

Go-to-Market Ideas

How to Sell Beacon to Agents

Channel 1: Brokerage Partnerships

Approach brokerage owners or managing brokers. Offer Beacon as a perk for their agents. Brokerages are always looking for ways to attract and retain agents. "Give your agents a marketing edge" is a compelling pitch. Volume discount: $99/agent/mo if the brokerage commits 10+ seats.

Channel 2: Agent Associations & Events

Austin Board of Realtors, local RE meetups, Inman Connect. Do a live demo: pull up an agent's real Google profile, seed Beacon on the spot, and show them a week's worth of content in 60 seconds. That demo closes deals.

Channel 3: Mortgage & Title Partnerships

Mortgage brokers and title companies have direct relationships with agents. Offer a referral fee ($50/signup) or co-branded version. "Powered by [Title Company]" — the title company looks innovative, you get distribution.

Channel 4: Cold Outreach to Top Producers

Use your own Beacon outreach tools to identify top-producing agents in Austin who have weak online presence. The pitch writes itself: "You closed $12M last year. Imagine what you'd do with consistent marketing."

What to Seed Into the Agent's Beacon

Context Onboarding Checklist

This is what makes Beacon write like THEIR agent, not "a real estate agent."

Agent Identity

  • Full bio + years in business
  • Specializations (luxury, first-time, investment, relocation)
  • Farm areas / neighborhoods they focus on
  • Team structure (solo, TC, showing agent, etc.)
  • Brokerage affiliation
  • Awards / designations (CRS, ABR, GRI, etc.)
  • Personal brand voice (professional vs. casual, humor style)

Market Context

  • Service area cities and zip codes
  • Typical price range
  • Current active listings (address, price, features)
  • Recent closed deals (for social proof)
  • Local market stats (from MLS or Redfin)
  • Key local amenities (schools, parks, restaurants)
  • Competitor agents (3-5 names + websites)

Past Client Database

  • Client names + close dates (for anniversary emails)
  • Property addresses (for neighborhood-specific content)
  • Referral sources (to nurture referral partners)
  • Client testimonials / review quotes

Brand Assets

  • Headshot / team photo
  • Logo
  • Brand colors
  • Website URL
  • Social media handles
  • Preferred hashtags
  • "Phrases I always use" and "Phrases to avoid"
