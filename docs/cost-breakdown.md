## Tax Estimates (Texas LLC / Sole Prop)

Texas has no state income tax, but there are federal and other obligations.

### Federal Taxes

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| Self-employment tax | **15.3%** | Social Security (12.4%) + Medicare (2.9%) on net profit. This is the big one. |
| Federal income tax | **10-24%** | Depends on total taxable income bracket. Most small businesses fall in 12-22%. |
| Quarterly estimated payments | — | Required if you expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes. Due Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15. |

### State & Local (Texas)

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| Texas state income tax | **0%** | Texas has no state income tax |
| Texas franchise tax | **0%** | No tax owed if revenue < $2.47M/year |
| Sales tax on services | **0%** | Web design / digital services are not taxable in Texas |

### Effective Tax Rate Estimate

| Annual Net Profit | Approx Effective Rate | Approx Tax Owed |
|---|---|---|
| $30,000 | ~25% | ~$7,500 |
| $50,000 | ~27% | ~$13,500 |
| $75,000 | ~29% | ~$21,750 |
| $100,000 | ~30% | ~$30,000 |
| $150,000 | ~32% | ~$48,000 |

*Effective rate = self-employment tax + federal income tax after standard deduction ($15,700 single / $31,400 married filing jointly for 2026). Actual rate depends on filing status, deductions, and other income.*

### Key Deductions to Track
- All infrastructure costs (Vercel, Neon, APIs)
- Software subscriptions (Claude, QuickBooks, Google Workspace)
- Home office (percentage of rent/mortgage, utilities, internet)
- Equipment (laptop, monitors, phone)
- Client domain purchases
- Marketing materials (flyers, business cards)
- Mileage for client visits (67 cents/mile for 2026)
- Health insurance premiums (self-employed deduction)
- Retirement contributions (SEP-IRA: up to 25% of net profit)

## Break-even Analysis

**Fixed monthly costs**: ~$139-169 (tools) + ~$39 (infra) = **~$178-208/mo**

**Break-even**: 4 Starter sites OR 2 Growth sites covers all fixed costs for the year.

Everything after that is margin.
