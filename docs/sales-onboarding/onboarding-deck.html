The flow

Seven stages from cold to closed.

Average cycle: 7–14 days for small deals, 14–30 for larger ones.

1

Source

Find the right businesses. GBP gaps, Yelp, drive-bys, referrals, industry-specific lists.

2

Outreach

One personalized email or walk-in. Three-touch follow-up cadence: day 0, day 4, day 11.

3

Discovery call

30 minutes, structured around five questions. Always book the next meeting before hanging up.

4

Audit & proposal

One-page audit + three-tier proposal. Sent 24 hours before review meeting, never without one scheduled.

5

Demo & review

Live walkthrough of a matching demo site, then proposal walk-through, then ask for the close.

6

Close

Signed agreement + 50% deposit cleared = sale. Verbal commits don't count.

7

Handoff

One-page handoff doc to fulfillment. Stay on the welcome thread until launch, then step out.

Stage 3 deep-dive

The five discovery questions.

Drive the conversation. These five questions in 30 minutes.

"Walk me through your business — how long, what you do, who your customers are."
"How are most new customers finding you today?"
"What's the biggest bottleneck right now — getting leads, converting them, or delivering the work?"
"If we fixed that, what would it be worth to you over a year?"
"What's stopped you from doing this before?"

Always close the discovery call by booking the next meeting:

"Let me put together a short audit and a proposal — three options at different price points so you can see what fits. I'll send it over by [day], and we'll book 20 minutes to walk through it. Sound good?"

Stage 5 deep-dive

The demo / proposal review.

Walk-through structure (20–30 min)

  • Recap what they told you (2 min)
  • Show a live demo in their vertical (5 min)
  • Walk the proposal (10 min)
  • Ask for the decision (5 min)
  • Handle objections (5 min)

Demo tips

  • Always share screen — don't just describe
  • Pull the demo closest to their industry
  • Show mobile view — most customers are on phones
  • For Beacon: log in and show real outputs

The closing line

"Which of the three feels right for where you are?"

Then stop talking. Silence is uncomfortable. Most people fill it with the answer. Don't rescue them with more options or more discounting.

Be ready

The four objections you'll hear most.

"It's too expensive."

"Most of our clients felt that way at first. The way to think about it: if one new customer a month covers the whole investment, what's the math on that for your business?" Then wait.

"I already have a website."

"When's the last time it brought you a real lead? Most sites still get traffic but don't convert. Want a free 15-minute audit?"

"Send me some info."

"Happy to. Quick question first — is now actually a bad time, or are you trying to politely tell me it's not for you? Either is fine."

"Can you guarantee leads / rankings?"

"No, and I wouldn't trust anyone who does. What I can guarantee is the work itself: site live in two weeks, SEO set up correctly, content on schedule. The leads follow when those are right."
