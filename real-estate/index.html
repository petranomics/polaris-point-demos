From the Editor

I sell the central pockets, on streets I've walked a thousand times.

Most agents in Austin will sell anything anywhere. I don't. I sell the streets between Lamar and Airport, between 35th and Cesar Chavez — Hyde Park, Tarrytown, Mueller, Bouldin, East Cesar Chavez, Travis Heights. I know which lots flood. I know which renovations were done well. I know which neighbors will tell you the truth about the house, and which won't.

This is the page I wish I'd had when I was buying my first house in 78751 in 2014. Field notes from a working agent — what's actually on market, what just sold and why, what the data says about the next ninety days, and the longer essays that come from being on the ground every week.

If you're thinking about buying or selling in central Austin, the listings below are real. The sold prices are real. The letters at the bottom are from real clients. Read what's useful, then write me a note. I'd rather have one honest conversation than ten polite ones.

— S.C.

The 1922 bungalow with the kitchen you'd actually cook in.

3812 Avenue G, Austin · MLS 9384701

4 Bed 3 Bath 2,418 SqFt 1922 Built

Original hardwoods through the main floor, restored casement windows, and a screened porch made for slow Sundays. The kitchen renovation pulled out the 1980s cabinets and gave it back a working pantry, marble counters, and a vintage stove that came with the house. Walkable to Quack's, Asti, and the Avenue B grocery.

The previous owner loved this house for thirty-one years. It's the kind of bones you can build a life around.
